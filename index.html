Alternative Proposal to XP Leaderboard

This report is a detailed proposal outlining an alternative to the current XP leaderboard system used within the platform. It presents observations, drawbacks, and user sentiment associated with the existing model and introduces a more purpose-driven initiative called “Under 25 Creators.”

The proposal aims to realign engagement strategies with the platform’s core objectives of helping students learn, earn, and communicate effectively. It also focuses on creating a healthy, inclusive, and motivational environment that encourages creativity, consistent participation, and meaningful recognition.

This report is structured into sections as follows:

  1. Introduction – Highlights the challenges with the current XP leaderboard system and its misalignment with the platform’s objectives.
  2. Proposed Initiative: Under 25 Creators – Introduces a new engagement model focused on creativity, authenticity, and community participation.
  3. Objective – Defines the goals of the initiative, including fostering learning, collaboration, and recognition among students.
  4. How It Works – Details participation, content creation, evaluation, and reward mechanisms.
  5. Comparison: XP Leaderboard vs Under 25 Creators Program – Provides a clear side-by-side analysis of the current and proposed systems.
  6. Benefits of the Under 25 Creators Initiative – Outlines advantages for users and the platform.
  7. Additional Suggestions – Recommends supplementary actions to enhance engagement and recognition.
  8. Final Take – Summarizes the rationale, potential impact, and long-term value of adopting the proposed initiative.

Introduction

In the previous report, I highlighted several issues with the current gamification system, particularly the XP leaderboard. While gamification can boost short-term engagement, it does not fully align with the long-term purpose of the platform.

Limited relevance to the target audience

The platform caters to students under 25 who come here to learn, earn, and communicate. The XP leaderboard does not effectively support these goals or contribute to meaningful growth for users.

Temporary engagement

Missions such as Strategize and Earn, Mega Reward Missions, Midnight Missions, and Surprise Missions have successfully brought excitement and competition to the app. However, the XP leaderboard has shown limited and inconsistent impact on overall participation.

Negative community perception

Over time, a mindset has developed that not participating in the leaderboard is a flex. This attitude goes against the values of inclusivity, motivation, and equal participation that the platform promotes.

Operational complexity

Evaluating every mission manually increases the workload on the evaluation team and raises the possibility of delays or errors. This affects fairness, transparency, and user trust in the system.

To address these concerns, this proposal suggests a more balanced and meaningful engagement model that sustains participation, encourages creativity, and supports the core mission of the platform.

Proposal: Under 25 Creators

The Under 25 Creators program highlights consistent and original content from students across different spaces. Top contributors are recognized through awards, badges, and community appreciation.

Objective

The objective of the Under 25 Creators program is to encourage students to create, share, and engage with original, high-quality content that reflects their ideas, creativity, and perspectives. This initiative focuses on building a culture of recognition, collaboration, and community while maintaining a healthy sense of competition.

How It Works

  • Interest Based Participation: Users select at least three spaces to participate in. One from City, one from Domain, and one from Entertainment.

  • Consistent Content Creation: Users post regularly in their chosen spaces. Evaluation will focus on content quality, originality, relatability, and contribution to the community.

  • Active Participation: Users participate in discussions, challenges, and activities within their spaces to remain active and engaged.

  • Authenticity: Genuine and creative posts are prioritized. Copied, low-effort, or AI-generated content will not be considered for rewards or recognition.

  • Community Voting and Evaluation: At the end of each season, members of each space vote for their favorite creators. Engagement data and quality metrics are used to ensure fair and transparent results.

  • Space Awards and Rewards: Winners in each space receive Space Awards and cash rewards, while runner ups are awarded certificates, fostering a sense of achievement, recognition, and belonging within the community.

  • Long Term Recognition: After several seasons, consistent contributors with high-quality and impactful content will receive a badge such as “Best Under 25 Creator.” This badge symbolizes creativity, reliability, and excellence within the community.

Benefits of the Under 25 Creators Initiative

  • Encourages students to learn, earn, and connect in a meaningful way.

  • Promotes authentic content creation instead of performative participation.

  • Builds a stronger and more inclusive student community.

  • Recognizes consistent effort and creativity with credibility and transparency.

  • Reduces workload for the evaluation team with a balanced review process.

  • Provides the platform with opportunities to showcase student creators on official channels.

  • Strengthens long-term user engagement and community loyalty.

Additional Suggestions

  • Share creator stories and achievements across official Under 25 social media pages.

  • Introduce mentorship or networking opportunities for top performers.

  • Promote ttt academy courses at affordable prices for students to learn and improvise content creation

Final Take

The Under 25 Creators initiative aligns perfectly with the vision of empowering young individuals to learn, earn, and express themselves. It promotes creativity, consistency, and collaboration while fostering an inclusive and motivating environment.

Recently, there has been a visible decline in active users, with many leaving due to dissatisfaction with the current leaderboard system or a lack of recognition. Genuine creators and writers are either not posting or not receiving the appreciation they deserve. A performative community cannot sustain long-term growth for a platform like Under 25, which has immense potential.

The community is made up of young people from diverse regions, languages, cultures, and skill sets, yet many genuine voices go unnoticed. A program like Under 25 Creators can help surface these talents and inspire more meaningful participation. Even a hybrid model combining aspects of the XP system and the Creators program could serve as a valuable experiment.

Under 25 has the potential to become a platform where the next generation of creators begins their journey. Exploring this idea could redefine how students express themselves, collaborate, and grow together in a supportive digital community.
