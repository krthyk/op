6 Underrated UPI Apps You Should Consider Trying in 2026

India’s digital payments ecosystem has evolved rapidly over the past decade. Unified Payments Interface, commonly known as UPI, has become the backbone of the country’s digital economy. According to data released by the National Payments Corporation of India, the system now processes more than 21 billion transactions every month and handles transactions worth over ₹28 lakh crore in value. The massive growth reflects how deeply UPI has become embedded in everyday life, from grocery purchases to online subscriptions and bill payments. While well-known apps such as PhonePe, Google Pay and Paytm dominate the market, there are several other applications offering unique features, rewards, or specialized financial services. Some of these apps focus on cashback campaigns, others integrate credit cards, lending services, or investment tools directly into the payment ecosystem. This article explores six lesser-known but promising UPI applications that offer useful features and may be worth trying if you are looking for alternatives beyond the most popular apps.

Navi

Source: Moneycontrol

Navi is a rapidly growing fintech platform founded by entrepreneur Sachin Bansal, the co-founder of Flipkart. The platform aims to simplify financial services through a mobile-first approach. The app integrates UPI payments with a range of financial products such as instant personal loans, health insurance and investment services. The interface is intentionally minimal, which makes it accessible even for users who are new to digital finance.

Features
  • > Fast UPI payments and bill payments
  • > Integrated personal loans and insurance
  • > Simple minimal interface
  • > Occasional cashback campaigns
Pros
  • ✓ Clean user interface
  • ✓ Quick transaction processing
Cons
  • × Cashback availability varies

Super.money

Source: LiveMint

Super.money is a relatively new digital payments platform backed by the Flipkart ecosystem. It gained attention soon after launch due to its focus on cashback rewards and promotional offers. The app is designed around frequent digital payments and aims to reward everyday transactions with small but consistent cashback incentives. Some campaigns also offer bonuses for merchant payments or referrals.

Features
  • > UPI scan and pay
  • > Cashback campaigns
  • > Credit integrations
  • > Merchant rewards
Pros
  • ✓ Frequent promotional offers
  • ✓ Clean modern design
Cons
  • × Rewards are usually small

Kredit.Pe

Source: Analytics Insight

Kredit.Pe is a fintech platform that blends UPI payments with credit-based rewards. One of its key differentiators is the integration of RuPay credit cards with UPI transactions, allowing users to earn rewards even when making regular payments.

Features
  • > UPI payments with rewards
  • > RuPay credit card integration
  • > Referral rewards
Pros
  • ✓ Credit card reward support
Cons
  • × Smaller user base

BHIM UPI

Source: NPCI

BHIM is the official UPI application developed by the National Payments Corporation of India. The app focuses primarily on secure bank-to-bank transfers and reliability rather than promotional rewards.

Features
  • > Direct bank-to-bank payments
  • > Simple and lightweight interface
  • > Multi-language support
Pros
  • ✓ Government backed
  • ✓ Reliable payment infrastructure
Cons
  • × Limited reward ecosystem

BharatPe

Source: Inc42

BharatPe is primarily known for enabling merchants to accept UPI payments through QR codes. Over time, the company has expanded into consumer services, lending products and rewards.

Features
  • > UPI payments
  • > Merchant QR payments
  • > Rewards ecosystem
Pros
  • ✓ Strong fintech infrastructure
Cons
  • × More merchant focused

CRED

Source: Moneycontrol

CRED is known for its premium user experience and initially gained popularity as a credit card bill payment platform. Over time, it expanded to include UPI payments, lending, and exclusive rewards.

Features
  • > Credit card bill tracking
  • > UPI payments
  • > Premium rewards marketplace
Pros
  • ✓ Excellent user interface
  • ✓ Premium reward ecosystem
Cons
  • × Many rewards are vouchers
Note: Apps like Paytm and Google Pay may not provide cashback frequently today. However, they still run promotional campaigns and seasonal offers that can be quite useful. If you want to maximize rewards or savings, it may still be worthwhile to check those apps occasionally.
