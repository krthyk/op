6 Underrated UPI Apps They Don't Want You to Know: Max Cashback and Many Other Benefits 🤫

8 min read March 2026 UPI · Fintech · India

Cast your mind back to 2017 or 2018. Every UPI transaction felt like a small lottery. Google Pay was handing out guaranteed cashback on every single payment. PhonePe had scratch cards that actually gave you money. Paytm was throwing promotional credits at you for just opening the app. Paying your electricity bill gave you Rs 50 back. Recharging your phone saved you Rs 10 every time. Those were genuinely good days for the average digital payment user.

Then things quietly changed. The scratch cards stayed, but the "Better Luck Next Time" messages started appearing more and more often. The guaranteed cashback turned into "up to" cashback, which in practice meant almost never. The promotional coupons shifted from direct money to brand vouchers, most of which expire before you remember to use them. Paytm cashback went into a Paytm wallet you could never quite spend properly. Google Pay rewards became collectibles and stamps. PhonePe started pushing insurance products instead.

And then came the platform fees. Recharging your prepaid number now costs an extra Rs 2 or Rs 3 in "convenience charges." Paying your electricity bill, broadband, or gas adds a processing fee on top. These fees are small individually, but if you are a regular user making 20 to 30 payments a month, you are effectively paying Rs 50 to Rs 100 extra every month just to use apps that used to reward you for doing the same thing.

Yet most of us have not switched. Why? Habit, mostly. PhonePe is already on your home screen. Your contacts are already linked on Google Pay. You know where the QR scanner button is. Change feels like effort, and for something as mundane as a payment app, the motivation rarely feels strong enough. The result is that the big three have effectively captured the market through inertia, not merit.

What very few people realise is that a new generation of UPI apps has arrived and they are actively competing for your attention with real cashback on almost every transaction, no platform fees on recharges and bills, free credit cards you can link to UPI, built-in credit lines, fixed deposit options, and gold purchases. These apps want to earn your habit from scratch, so they are putting real money on the table to do it.

A practical tip if you use UPI heavily: do not pick just one of these apps. Rotate between two or three of them depending on which is running the best cashback at a given time. The rewards compound meaningfully over a month of regular transactions. The six apps below are worth bookmarking and trying, even if just for a week.

1. Navi

4.5/5 (4.66M+ Play Store reviews)

Navi is a rapidly growing fintech platform founded by Sachin Bansal, the co-founder of Flipkart, along with Ankit Agarwal, launched in December 2018 under Navi Technologies Limited. The platform was built with a single guiding philosophy: making financial services simple, affordable, and universally accessible through a mobile-first approach. What distinguishes Navi from most other UPI apps is its genuine integration of financial products alongside payments. Rather than treating UPI as an isolated feature, Navi weaves together instant personal loans, comprehensive health insurance, and investment services into one clean interface.

As of early 2025, Navi has emerged as the fourth-largest UPI app in India by transaction volume, processing over 260 million transactions in a single month, a figure that places it remarkably close behind the big three. The app is NPCI-approved and currently holds the number 2 position in the Free Finance category on the Google Play Store. Its UPI feature works across all linked bank accounts and supports bill payments, merchant QR scans, and person-to-person transfers.

Key Features
  • Fast UPI payments and QR scan-and-pay
  • Instant personal loans up to Rs 20 lakh
  • Health insurance up to Rs 3 crore coverage
  • Cashless claims at 12,000+ network hospitals
  • Home loans up to Rs 5 crore (select cities)
  • Referral rewards programme
  • Minimal, clutter-free interface
  • Bill payments and utility recharges

Pros

  • Exceptionally clean and minimal UI
  • Integrated loans, insurance and UPI in one app
  • Strong loan eligibility from Rs 3L annual income
  • NPCI approved and RBI regulated products
  • Fast transaction processing speed
  • Occasional cashback campaigns for users

Cons

  • Cashback offers are infrequent and inconsistent
  • Credit line closure process is slow and offline
  • Max credit line transaction limit is only Rs 2,000
  • Home loans limited to select cities only

2. Super.money

4.6/5 (986K+ Play Store reviews)

Super.money is Flipkart Group's bold attempt to build India's most rewarding UPI platform. Unlike conventional UPI apps that hand out scratch cards and coins with dubious value, super.money is structured around a credit-first philosophy, offering guaranteed real cashback on every transaction, every time. The app was formally launched in 2024 and by early 2025 had already surpassed CRED to become the fifth-largest UPI app in India by transaction volume, processing over 139 million transactions in a single month.

Its headline product, the superCard, issued in partnership with Utkarsh Small Finance Bank, brings a credit card natively onto UPI rails, enabling users to earn cashback while building their credit score simultaneously. The app also offers superFD, a fixed deposit product with interest rates up to 9.5% per annum, with all deposits insured by DICGC up to Rs 5 lakh. The app partners with Yes Bank for UPI infrastructure and is fully NPCI-approved. Supported merchants include Flipkart, Amazon, Swiggy, Zomato, BlinkIt, Uber, Ola, BookMyShow, and hundreds more.

Key Features
  • Guaranteed cashback on every UPI payment (up to 5%)
  • superCard: RuPay credit card on UPI rails
  • superFD: Fixed deposits at up to 9.5% p.a.
  • superCash: Instant pre-approved loans from 10.49%
  • Default camera scan mode for faster payments
  • Instant balance check without banking passwords
  • Wide merchant support across Flipkart, Amazon, Swiggy
  • Robust security for payments and personal data

Pros

  • Genuine, transparent cashback with no scratch cards
  • Credit card integrated with UPI, a first in India
  • SuperFD offers 9.5% interest, DICGC insured
  • Clean, modern, and intuitive interface
  • Credit building for all users from Rs 100 onwards
  • Fast-growing merchant acceptance network

Cons

  • Bill payment features are limited vs older apps
  • Cashback rate drops for smaller transactions below Rs 500
  • Supercard eligibility may show waitlists
  • Relatively newer, some features still maturing
